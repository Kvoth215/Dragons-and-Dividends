1. Overview & Classroom Setup

Life Quest is a browser-based personal finance simulation played individually. Students choose a life path, manage a monthly budget over 12 simulated months, respond to random events (dice rolls), and must complete subject-area quests to advance. Progress saves automatically to the student's Claude.ai session.

Suggested Pacing (adjust to your schedule):

  • Session 1 (30–45 min): Intro, character creation, Months 1–2. Discuss life path choices as a class first.
  • Sessions 2–4: Months 3–8. Pause at quest completion for class discussion.
  • Session 5+: Months 9–12 and final results. Debrief & compare outcomes.
  • Year 2 (optional, advanced): Unlocks automatically after Year 1 completion.
💡 Teaching tip: Before students begin, ask the class: "If you had to pick a life path today, which would you choose — and why?" The subsequent discussion previews nearly every standard in 17.2 (Income) organically.

3. Year 1 Quest Answers (Months 1–11)

Quest 1 — The Merchant's Vault  Month 1
🔢 Math PA 17.4.6-8.C

Unlocks: Savings account feature. Students cannot save gold until this quest is passed.

Question: You deposit 500gp at 4% annual interest. How many gold coins after 2 years?

Year 1:  500 × 1.04 = 520gp
Year 2:  520 × 1.04 = 540.8 ≈ 541gp

ANSWER: 541gp  (accepted range: 539–543gp)

Formula used: A = P(1 + r)^t  →  500(1.04)² = 500 × 1.0816 = 540.8
💬 Discussion: "Why do banks pay you interest? What does the bank DO with your money while it's there?" Extend: What if the rate was 8%? What if you waited 10 years instead of 2?
Quest 2 — The Budget Proclamation  Month 2
📜 ELA PA 17.3.9-12.E

Task: Write 4–6 sentences covering: (1) income source, (2) two biggest expenses, (3) savings amount and reason, (4) a specific 3-month financial goal.

This is a writing quest — no single correct answer.
See ELA Rubric (Section 7) for scoring guidance.

REQUIRED ELEMENTS TO VERIFY:
  ✅ Specific income source named (not just "money from work")
  ✅ Two largest expenses identified (should be Lodgings + Tax or Provisions)
  ✅ Savings amount stated WITH a reason (e.g., "I am saving 200gp/month
     because I want to build an emergency fund for unexpected events")
  ✅ 3-month goal is specific and measurable (includes a number!)

TEACHER NOTE: Students who write "I want to save money" without specifying
how much or why have NOT met the standard. Return for revision.

Gold reward: 75gp (auto-awarded upon checklist confirmation)
Stamp for review: YES — collect or discuss with student.
💬 Discussion: "What's the difference between 'I want to save money' and 'I will save 200gp/month for 3 months to reach 600gp'?" Connect to SMART goal-setting.
Quest 3 — The Scarcity Scroll  Month 3
🗺️ Social Studies PA 17.1.6-8.B & 17.3.6-8.D

Question: Provisions cost 20% more. Income 3,000gp, goal: save 500gp. How much must other expenses be cut?

Original provisions:    700gp
New provisions (×1.20): 840gp  (+140gp increase)

Original total expenses: 2,500gp
New total expenses:      2,640gp

Maximum spending = Income − Savings goal = 3,000 − 500 = 2,500gp
Must cut: 2,640 − 2,500 = 140gp

ANSWER: 140gp  (accepted range: 135–145gp)

This 140gp is the OPPORTUNITY COST of the drought —
something else must be given up because provisions cost more.
💬 Discussion: "What would you cut first — transportation or entertainment? Why do different people make different choices?" This opens PA 17.1.6-8.B (opportunity cost differs by person/situation).
Quest 4 — The Candle Conspiracy  Month 4
⚗️ Science PA 17.3.9-12.K

Question: Candles: 2gp each, 4-hour life, use 4hrs/day. Crystal Lamp: 80gp upfront, 0gp/day. Break-even in how many days?

Candle use: 4 hrs/day ÷ 4 hrs/candle = 1 candle/day → 2gp/day
Crystal Lamp cost stays fixed at 80gp (one-time purchase)

Break-even equation:
  Candle total cost = Lamp total cost
  2gp × days = 80gp
  days = 80 ÷ 2 = 40 days

ANSWER: 40 days  (accepted range: 38–42 days)

After day 40, the Crystal Lamp costs less every subsequent day.
The Candle Guild's objections are financially irrelevant after day 40.
💬 Science connection: Compare to real-world LED vs. incandescent bulb math. EPA data: LED lasts ~25x longer. Have students calculate the real-world break-even for their classroom lights.
Quest 5 — The Discount Dungeon  Month 5
🔢 Math PA 17.3.6-8.B & 17.3.6-8.L

Question: Armor costs 800gp. Apply 25% discount, then 8% sales tax. Final price?

Step 1 — Apply discount FIRST:
  Discount amount:    800 × 0.25 = 200gp off
  Discounted price:   800 − 200 = 600gp

Step 2 — Apply tax to DISCOUNTED price:
  Tax amount:         600 × 0.08 = 48gp
  Final price:        600 + 48 = 648gp

ANSWER: 648gp  (accepted range: 645–651gp)

COMMON ERROR: Applying tax to the original 800gp price (gives 666gp — wrong).
Tax is always calculated on the price actually paid.
Order matters. In life and in math.
💬 Discussion: "Which is better for the shopper — getting the discount before tax, or the tax before the discount? Does it actually matter mathematically?" (It doesn't — but let students discover this!)
Quest 6 — The Artificer's Dilemma  Month 6
⚗️ Science PA 17.3.9-12.K

Question: Horse (30gp/mo + 200gp/yr) vs. Steam Cart (50gp/mo + 100gp/yr). Which is cheaper over 3 years, and by how much?

Horse total:
  Monthly: 30 × 36 months = 1,080gp
  Annual:  200 × 3 years  =   600gp
  TOTAL:                     1,680gp

Steam Cart total:
  Monthly: 50 × 36 months = 1,800gp
  Annual:  100 × 3 years  =   300gp
  TOTAL:                     2,100gp

Difference: 2,100 − 1,680 = 420gp

ANSWER: Horse is cheaper, by 420gp  (accepted: Horse + 415–425gp)

Concept: Total Cost of Ownership — the true cost of a purchase includes
all ongoing costs, not just the purchase price. Real-world: car leases,
phone plans, subscriptions all use this principle against consumers.
💬 Science/Math connection: "What other factors should be considered that we didn't calculate?" (reliability, resale value, environmental impact, depreciation). PA 17.3.9-12.A — informed spending decisions.
Quest 7 — The Complaint Scroll  Month 7
📜 ELA PA 17.6.6-8.G

Task: Write a formal complaint (4–6 sentences): what was purchased/promised, what was received, resolution requested, why consumer protection matters.

REQUIRED ELEMENTS TO VERIFY:
  ✅ Product/service named AND what was specifically promised
  ✅ What was actually received — the gap between promise and reality
  ✅ Specific resolution requested (refund? replacement? apology? all three?)
  ✅ Connection to why consumer rights/fair business practices exist

QUALITY INDICATORS:
  Strong: "I request a full refund of 200gp within 14 days, as the
          Consumer Protection Charter requires merchants to honor product claims."
  Weak:   "I want my money back because it was unfair."

The goal is formal register and specific claims — not emotional venting.
This mirrors real consumer complaint letters (FTC, BBB, etc.)

Gold reward: 90gp (auto-awarded upon checklist confirmation)
💬 Real-world extension: Show students the FTC complaint process at reportfraud.ftc.gov. Have them identify which consumer protection law applies to their fictional scroll.
Quest 8 — The Moneylender's Maze  Month 8
🔢 Math PA 17.6.6-8.E

Question: Felgar: 600gp at 5%/mo for 6 months, 115gp/mo payment. Roderick: 600gp + 10% fee, repay 660gp total. How much more does Felgar cost?

Friendly Felgar total:
  115gp × 6 payments = 690gp

Reasonable Roderick total:
  600gp + (600 × 0.10) = 600 + 60 = 660gp

Difference: 690 − 660 = 30gp

ANSWER: Felgar costs 30gp more  (accepted range: 27–33gp)

KEY INSIGHT: Monthly rates that "look small" compound quickly.
5%/month sounds modest. But 5% × 12 = 60% annual rate.
This is predatory lending territory. Roderick's "10% fee" = ~20% annual.
Both are expensive — Roderick is merely the lesser of two evils.

Real-world: payday loans average 400% APR. Credit cards: 20–30% APR.
💬 Discussion: "Which moneylender sounds friendlier in their ad? Which IS friendlier mathematically?" This is exactly how predatory lenders work — friendly branding, brutal math.
Quest 9 — The King's Ledger  Month 9
🗺️ Social Studies PA 17.3.9-12.L

Question: Kingdom collects 10,000gp. Roads 25%, Healthcare 30%, Defense 20%, Schools 15%, Remaining ?%. How much goes to Schools?

Schools of Learning:
  10,000 × 0.15 = 1,500gp

ANSWER: 1,500gp  (accepted range: 1,450–1,550gp)

BONUS (no calculation needed):
  Remaining %: 100 − 25 − 30 − 20 − 15 = 10%
  Remaining gold: 10,000 × 0.10 = 1,000gp (the Royal Reserve)

REAL-WORLD CONNECTION:
  PA state budget spends roughly 40% on education, 35% on health/human
  services, 15% on debt/pensions, 10% on other programs.
  Ask: Is 15% on schools enough? What would students cut or add?
💬 Discussion: "If you were the King and had to cut one category by 5%, which would you cut — and what would the consequences be?" This is real budget trade-off thinking (PA 17.1.6-8.B).
Quest 10 — The Sunstone Summit  Month 10
⚗️ Science PA 17.3.9-12.K

Question: Coalstone 80gp/mo, Sunstone 20gp/mo + 1,200gp setup. Break-even in how many months?

Monthly savings by switching to Sunstone:
  80 − 20 = 60gp saved per month

Break-even calculation:
  Setup cost ÷ Monthly savings = 1,200 ÷ 60 = 20 months

ANSWER: 20 months  (accepted range: 19–21 months)

After month 20, Sunstone saves 60gp every single month — forever.
Over 10 years (120 months): saves 6,000gp total.
The 1,200gp investment returns 5× in a decade.

REAL-WORLD CONNECTION:
  Average US home solar payback: 6–12 years.
  Average monthly savings after payback: $50–$150.
  This is why renewable energy is increasingly a financial decision,
  not just an environmental one. The Coalstone Guild's days are numbered.
💬 Science extension: Research current Pennsylvania solar incentives and rebate programs. Calculate whether solar would break even faster with a 30% federal tax credit applied.
Quest 11 — The Final Chronicle  Month 11
📜 ELA PA 17.1.9-12.A

Task: Financial reflection (5–7 sentences): best decision, a challenge/surprise, one thing to do differently, specific goal for next year (with number).

REQUIRED ELEMENTS TO VERIFY:
  ✅ Best decision identified AND explained (not just named — why did it work?)
  ✅ Challenge/surprise described with specific budget impact (how many gp?)
  ✅ "Would do differently" is substantive and reasoned, not vague
  ✅ Next-year goal is SPECIFIC and NUMBERED ("save 3,000gp" not "save more")

HIGHEST-QUALITY RESPONSES will:
  — Connect a random event to a financial concept (e.g., "The dragon attack
    taught me why emergency funds matter, so next year I'll save 200gp/mo
    into my Vault before spending any disposable gold")
  — Show cause-and-effect thinking about their own decisions
  — Set a goal that responds to their actual Year 1 results

This is the summative writing assessment for Year 1.
Strongly consider requiring students to read this aloud or share in pairs.

Gold reward: 150gp (auto-awarded upon checklist confirmation)
💡 Assessment idea: Collect Final Chronicles as a portfolio piece. Compare to Budget Proclamation (Quest 2) to show growth in financial vocabulary and thinking sophistication over the year.

4. Year 2 Hard Mode Quest Answers (Months 13–17)

Year 2 unlocks automatically after completing Year 1. Income increases 5% (modest raise) but rent rises 200gp (landlord), creating tighter margins. Events are more volatile. Five new quests with harder math and deeper writing prompts.

Quest 12 — The Debt Dragon  Month 13
🔢 Math PA 17.6.9-12.E

Question: You borrowed 5,000gp at 18% annual interest. You pay only the minimum (2% of balance/month). How much do you owe after 3 months?

Monthly interest rate: 18% ÷ 12 = 1.5% per month

Month 1:
  Interest:      5,000 × 0.015 =   75gp
  Min. payment:  5,000 × 0.02  =  100gp
  Paid toward principal: 100 − 75 = 25gp
  New balance:   5,000 − 25    = 4,975gp

Month 2:
  Interest:      4,975 × 0.015 = 74.63gp ≈ 75gp
  Min. payment:  4,975 × 0.02  = 99.50gp ≈ 100gp
  Paid toward principal: ≈ 25gp
  New balance:   ≈ 4,950gp

Month 3:
  New balance:   ≈ 4,925gp

ANSWER: Approximately 4,925gp  (accepted range: 4,900–4,950gp)

KEY LESSON: After 3 months paying 300gp, balance only dropped 75gp.
That is 225gp paid in interest — 75% of payments went to interest.
This is the minimum payment trap. The Debt Dragon eats most of your gold.
💬 Discussion: "At this rate, how many years to pay off 5,000gp?" (Answer: over 30 years). Credit card minimum payment traps are the #1 personal finance trap for young adults.
Quest 13 — The Portfolio Parchment  Month 14
📜 ELA PA 17.4.9-12.H

Task: Write an investment portfolio recommendation (5–7 sentences) for a 28-year-old with moderate risk tolerance, 30-year time horizon, and 500gp/month to invest.

REQUIRED ELEMENTS TO VERIFY:
  ✅ At least TWO different investment types mentioned (stocks, bonds,
     mutual funds, index funds, real estate, etc.)
  ✅ Reasoning connects to time horizon (30 years = can weather volatility)
  ✅ Risk tolerance addressed explicitly — "moderate" means neither all stocks
     nor all bonds; some balance explained
  ✅ Diversification concept present (not all eggs in one basket)

QUALITY INDICATORS:
  Strong: "With a 30-year horizon, I recommend 70% in a broad stock index
          fund (higher growth potential, time to recover from downturns),
          25% in bond funds (stability and income), and 5% in cash savings
          for flexibility. At 500gp/month, compound growth over 30 years
          at 7% average return would grow to approximately 566,000gp."
  Weak:   "Put it in stocks because they make money."

NOTE: Students may not know exact return rates — that's fine. The reasoning
process is the assessment target. PA 17.4.9-12.H is about recommendation
quality, not calculation precision.

Gold reward: 175gp
Quest 14 — The Great Career Calculus  Month 15
🗺️ Social Studies PA 17.2.9-12.C

Question: Path A earns 38,000gp/yr with no debt. Path B earns 58,000gp/yr with 45,000gp debt (repaid at 500gp/mo). How many years until Path B's cumulative net earnings exceed Path A's?

Path A net earnings per year: 38,000gp (no debt costs)

Path B net earnings per year: 58,000 − (500 × 12) = 58,000 − 6,000 = 52,000gp
(during repayment period)

Debt payoff time: 45,000 ÷ 6,000/yr ≈ 7.5 years

During repayment (Years 1–7.5):
  Path A cumulative: 38,000 × 7.5 = 285,000gp
  Path B cumulative: 52,000 × 7.5 = 390,000gp
  
Path B is ALREADY ahead by 105,000gp after 7.5 years.

ANSWER: Path B exceeds Path A immediately — from Year 1.
(52,000 > 38,000 even with debt payments)

NUANCE QUESTION FOR CLASS: What if Path B's income was only 42,000gp?
  Path B net: 42,000 − 6,000 = 36,000/yr (LESS than Path A during repayment)
  Break-even: Path B catches up after debt is paid off (~year 10)

This demonstrates why income LEVEL matters as much as debt AMOUNT.
💬 Discussion: "Under what circumstances does the expensive education NOT pay off?" Connect to PA data on earnings by credential level (PA Dept. of Labor publishes this annually).
Quest 15 — The Tax Tribunal  Month 16
🔢 Math PA 17.2.9-12.H & 17.3.9-12.L

Question: Gross income 54,000gp/yr. Federal tax: 10% on first 11,000; 12% on 11,001–44,725; 22% on amount above 44,725. Calculate total federal tax owed.

This is a PROGRESSIVE (graduated) tax bracket calculation.
Each bracket taxes only the income IN that range.

Bracket 1 (10%):  11,000 × 0.10          = 1,100gp
Bracket 2 (12%):  (44,725 − 11,000) × 0.12
                = 33,725 × 0.12           = 4,047gp
Bracket 3 (22%):  (54,000 − 44,725) × 0.22
                =  9,275 × 0.22           = 2,040.50gp ≈ 2,041gp

TOTAL FEDERAL TAX: 1,100 + 4,047 + 2,041 = 7,188gp

ANSWER: 7,188gp  (accepted range: 7,100–7,275gp)

Effective tax rate: 7,188 ÷ 54,000 = 13.3%
(Much lower than the 22% marginal rate — a common misconception!)

COMMON STUDENT ERROR: Applying 22% to the entire 54,000 (gives 11,880 — wrong).
This is the most important thing to correct. The 22% bracket only applies
to income ABOVE $44,725.
💡 PA connection: Pennsylvania has a flat state income tax of 3.07% (as of 2024). Have students add this to their federal calculation for a realistic total tax burden.
Quest 16 — The Net Worth Reckoning  Month 17
🔢 Math PA 17.4.9-12.A

Question: Calculate net worth. Assets: Home 180,000gp, Car 12,000gp, Vault Savings 8,500gp, Investment Account 22,000gp. Liabilities: Mortgage 145,000gp, Car Loan 7,200gp, Credit Card Debt 2,400gp.

ASSETS (what you own):
  Home value:           180,000gp
  Car:                   12,000gp
  Vault Savings:          8,500gp
  Investment Account:    22,000gp
  TOTAL ASSETS:         222,500gp

LIABILITIES (what you owe):
  Mortgage:             145,000gp
  Car Loan:               7,200gp
  Credit Card:            2,400gp
  TOTAL LIABILITIES:    154,600gp

NET WORTH = Total Assets − Total Liabilities
          = 222,500 − 154,600 = 67,900gp

ANSWER: 67,900gp  (accepted range: 67,500–68,300gp)

INTERPRETATION GUIDE:
  Positive net worth = you own more than you owe ✅
  The home equity (180,000 − 145,000 = 35,000gp) is the largest
  component — which is typical for most middle-class Americans.

Extension: If home values DROP 10%, what happens to net worth?
  New home value: 162,000. New net worth: 62,500 − 7,400 = 49,900gp.
  A 10% home price drop causes a 26% net worth drop. Housing risk is real.
💬 Discussion: "Is a high net worth always good? What if all your net worth is tied up in your home and it's not liquid?" Connect to the 2008 housing crisis.

6. Cross-Curricular Connections

🔢 Math Teacher Notes

Quests 1, 5, 8, 12, 15, 16 are yours. All use arithmetic through pre-algebra. Quest 12 introduces iterative calculations (minimum payment trap) — good for spreadsheet extension. Quest 15 (tax brackets) is the most complex — preview with a simpler 2-bracket example first.

📜 ELA Teacher Notes

Quests 2, 7, 11, 13 are yours. Focus on formal register, specific claims, and evidence-based reasoning. The Complaint Scroll (Quest 7) connects to argument writing standards. Final Chronicle (Quest 11) is reflective narrative — consider pairing with a goal-setting journal unit.

🗺️ Social Studies Teacher Notes

Quests 3, 9, 14 are yours. Quest 3 (scarcity) pairs directly with any economics unit. Quest 9 (government budgets) connects to civics and public policy. Quest 14 (career ROI) is excellent for career exploration units — pair with PA labor market data.

⚗️ Science Teacher Notes

Quests 4, 6, 10 are yours. Quest 4 (candle break-even) connects to energy units. Quest 6 (total cost of ownership) connects to engineering design trade-offs. Quest 10 (renewable energy break-even) pairs with any energy sources unit — use real PA solar data for extension.

8. Discussion Questions by Month

  • Month 1 (After Character Creation): "Did anyone choose the same path for different reasons? What does that tell us about financial decisions?"
  • Month 2 (After First Random Event): "Did your event help or hurt? How prepared were you? What would an emergency fund have changed?"
  • Month 4 (Mid-point check): "Compare net worth across the class. Why are some paths ahead — is it income, or spending, or luck?"
  • Month 6 (After Artificer's Dilemma): "When is the expensive option actually the cheaper one? Give a real-world example."
  • Month 9 (After King's Ledger): "What should the government prioritize spending on? What would you cut from the royal budget?"
  • Month 12 (Final Results): "Who had the highest net worth? Is that person necessarily the 'winner'? What other measures of success matter?"
  • Post-Game: "If you could replay this with a different life path, which would you choose — and would it be different from your real-life plan?"

9. Differentiation Strategies

For Students Who Need Support:

  • Allow calculator use on all math quests (the concept, not the computation, is the standard)
  • Pair students to discuss events before writing responses
  • Pre-teach key vocabulary: interest, principal, opportunity cost, net worth
  • The Bard path's variable income can be confusing — suggest Knight or Artisan for struggling students

For Advanced Students:

  • Assign Year 2 Hard Mode as enrichment
  • Have students create their own random event cards with correct dollar amounts and PA standard citations
  • Extension: Build a spreadsheet to track their actual in-game data month by month
  • Compare outcomes: play the same path twice and see how different random events change the result
